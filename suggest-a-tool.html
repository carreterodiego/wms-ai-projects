Suggest a Tool

This hub is a shared resource. If you come across an AI tool that might be useful for teaching, assessment, or research at WMS, flag it here. No formal write-up needed — just a name and a line on what it does. We review every suggestion and add assessed tools to the external tools page.

How to suggest a tool

The bar is intentionally low. We're not asking for a full review — just enough to know what to look at. A useful suggestion includes:

  • Tool name — and a link if you have one
  • What it does — one or two sentences is enough
  • Why you think it's relevant — which part of the WMS workflow it might fit: marking, feedback, research, teaching, student support
  • Optional: whether you've tried it and what your initial impression was

We will acknowledge every suggestion, and if a tool is assessed and added to the hub, the person who flagged it will be credited.

What happens after you suggest a tool

  • Every suggestion is reviewed — usually within a week of the next working session
  • If a tool is relevant, it gets a short assessment: what it does, where it adds value, what its limits are, and how it relates to the WMS GPT toolkit
  • Assessed tools are added to the external tools section of the projects page with full notes
  • If a tool is not relevant to WMS, you'll hear back with a brief explanation
  • No suggestion leads to a tool being formally adopted or recommended without a broader conversation — the assessment is the first step, not the last
