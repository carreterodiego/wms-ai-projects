Custom GPTs at MIT Sloan: A Comprehensive Guide
MIT Sloan EdTech · Practitioner guide
Practice Guide		 Custom GPTs can be built without coding using OpenAI's GPT builder. Effectiveness improves through iterative prompt refinement and uploading domain-specific knowledge files. Best-in-class use case is a tutoring agent that gathers rubric context, reviews the assignment, and provides annotated feedback. Data privacy is a hard constraint — no PII in knowledge bases.
Grading Agent LinkValidates the two-document architecture (System Prompt + Knowledge Base). The iterative build-and-preview loop mirrors agent development practice. Confirms rubric upload as a core mechanism for alignment.

Generative AI in Higher Education: Seeing ChatGPT Through Universities' Policies, Resources, and Guidelines
Wang et al. · Policy analysis
Literature Review		 None of the top 100 US universities ban GenAI. 54.8% delegate decisions to instructors; 35.6% remain undecided. Key recommendations: accept AI's presence, align with learning objectives, evolve curriculum, and use multi-method assessment.
Grading Agent LinkSupports the "instructor-as-decision-maker" framing for agent deployment. Useful context for positioning agents as academic support instruments, not automated replacements.

Prompting With Purpose: The FRAME Model for Critical AI Use
Dr Dannie Jefferies · University of Waikato (WMS)
Framework		 FRAME is a five-element prompt structure: Frame the LLM's role, Relevant Knowledge, Approach, Mode (output format), and Explain Limits. Each element is grounded in peer-reviewed prompt engineering research. Designed for MNGMT563 at WMS.
Grading Agent LinkDirect design reference for agent system prompts — especially Role, structured reasoning, output format, and self-caveat requirements. The "Explain Limits" element maps onto band-anchoring and uncertainty-flagging logic.

Grading the Graders: Comparing Generative AI and Human Assessment in Essay Evaluation
Wetzler et al. · Teaching of Psychology, 2025
Empirical Study		 AI grading showed reasonable alignment with human graders on structured rubrics but diverged on subjective dimensions. Human graders are susceptible to halo effect, grade inflation, and fatigue. AI is best positioned as a supplement for consistency, not a replacement for nuanced evaluation.
Grading Agent LinkEmpirical support for the "AI as consistency tool" framing. Validates the Marking Consistency GPT premise while flagging where human review remains essential.

Implementation Considerations for Automated AI Grading of Student Work
Mixed-methods practitioner study
Practice Guide		 Teachers valued AI-generated narrative feedback more than numeric scores. Teacher mediation was essential when comments and grades diverged. Students wanted human involvement even when AI feedback was accurate. Design requirements: teacher oversight, clear interfaces, and support for iterative revision.
Grading Agent LinkStrong support for the Feedback Enhancer GPT concept. Narrative feedback is more valued than numeric scores — validates the agent architecture that produces structured qualitative commentary.

Henadirage, Chathuranga & Gunarathne · IJETHE, 2026
Empirical Study		 Academics reached modification/augmentation level for assessment design and feedback, but only substitution level for grading. Normative pressure is the primary adoption driver. Uncertainty about institutional permission is a significant barrier.

The Application of GPT-4 in Grading Design University Students' Assignments
Huang, Willems & Poon · IJTDE, 2026
Empirical Study		 Custom GPT-4 with rubrics and a worked exemplar achieved ICC > 0.7 with human instructors. Without rubrics: poor reliability. With rubrics + exemplar: best result (ICC ≈ 0.73). Intra-rater overall ICC across all rounds = 0.9074.
Grading Agent LinkDirect empirical validation for the iterative refinement method and rubric-plus-exemplar design. Confirms that a single good example in agent instructions is a meaningful reliability lever.

A Comprehensive Review of AI-Powered Grading and Tailored Feedback in Universities
Deepshikha · Discover Artificial Intelligence, 2025
Literature Review		 Narrative review of 77 studies (2018–2025). AI grading reduces workload, improves consistency, and enables faster feedback. Critical challenges: algorithmic bias, data privacy, lack of transparency, need for human oversight. Transformative only when embedded in human-mediated processes.
Grading Agent LinkBroad evidentiary base for the grading agent project. Emphasis on human oversight reinforces the "AI as co-grader, not autonomous grader" positioning.

Toward AI Grading of Student Problem Solutions in Introductory Physics: A Feasibility Study
Kortemeyer · Phys. Rev. Physics Education Research, 2023
Empirical Study		 GPT-4 + MathPix workflow achieved R² = 0.84 against human graders on a synthetic dataset. Promising for formative feedback; not reliable enough for final grade decisions without human review. GPT-4 requires no problem-specific training.
Grading Agent LinkR² = 0.84 is a useful reliability benchmark. The "formative feedback, not final grades" conclusion is the defensible positioning for WMS agents too.

Critical Thinking Assessment in Higher Education: A Mixed-Methods Comparative Analysis of AI and Human Evaluators
Trikoili et al. · Int. Journal of Human-Computer Interaction, 2024
Empirical Study		 ChatGPT 4.0 assessed critical thinking tasks. Absolute agreement ICC = 0.431 — not reliable enough for standalone grading. ChatGPT consistently assigned higher scores than the human grader and was more lenient with partial answers.
Grading Agent LinkKey caution: AI leniency inflation is a documented pattern on open-response critical thinking tasks. Locked band values and structural gates directly address this tendency.

Leveraging Prompt-Based LLMs for Automated Scoring and Feedback Generation in Higher Education
AlGhamdi et al. · Computers & Education, 2026
Prompt Engineering		 Prompt composition vs. decomposition and task order both affect scoring accuracy. No single configuration dominated across all students. Confidence scoring (flagging low-certainty outputs for human review) is a promising safeguard.
Grading Agent LinkTask order combined with prompt structure is the key variable. A potential future improvement: testing whether separating scoring and feedback generation steps improves accuracy for stronger submissions.

Making Sense of Generative AI for Assessments: Contrasting Student Claims and Assessor Evaluations
Fischer et al. · Int. Journal of Management Education, 2024
Empirical Study		 Management education context. Only ~10% of students reached the "critiquing" stage of sensemaking. Students overestimated their critical engagement with AI. Authentic sensemaking-oriented assessments are the best design response — not banning GenAI.
Grading Agent LinkThe 10% critiquing figure is a useful benchmark for calibrating grade distribution expectations in management programmes. The sensemaking framework also informs rubric design.
