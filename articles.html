Custom GPTs at MIT Sloan: A Comprehensive Guide
MIT Sloan EdTech · Practitioner guide
Practice Guide		 Custom GPTs can be built without coding using OpenAI's GPT builder. Effectiveness improves through iterative prompt refinement and uploading domain-specific knowledge files. Best-in-class use case is a tutoring agent that gathers rubric context, reviews the assignment, and provides annotated feedback. Data privacy is a hard constraint — no PII in knowledge bases. Wang et al. · Policy analysis

Dr Dannie Jefferies · University of Waikato (WMS)

Grading the Graders: Comparing Generative AI and Human Assessment in Essay Evaluation
Wetzler et al. · Teaching of Psychology, 2025
Empirical Study		 AI grading showed reasonable alignment with human graders on structured rubrics but diverged on subjective dimensions. Human graders are susceptible to halo effect, grade inflation, and fatigue. AI is best positioned as a supplement for consistency, not a replacement for nuanced evaluation. Download

Implementation Considerations for Automated AI Grading of Student Work
Mixed-methods practitioner study
Practice Guide		 Teachers valued AI-generated narrative feedback more than numeric scores. Teacher mediation was essential when comments and grades diverged. Students wanted human involvement even when AI feedback was accurate. Design requirements: teacher oversight, clear interfaces, and support for iterative revision. Download

Huang, Willems & Poon · IJTDE, 2026
Empirical Study		 Custom GPT-4 with rubrics and a worked exemplar achieved ICC > 0.7 with human instructors. Without rubrics: poor reliability. With rubrics + exemplar: best result (ICC ≈ 0.73). Intra-rater overall ICC across all rounds = 0.9074. Download

How Consistent Are Humans When Grading Programming Assignments?
Messer, Brown, Kölling & Shi · ACM Transactions on Computing Education, 2025
Empirical Study		 28 participants each graded 40 introductory programming assignments across correctness, code elegance, readability and documentation. Inter-rater reliability was very low (average Krippendorff's α = 0.2 for correctness, below 0.1 for other criteria). Individual consistency was also poor — only 1 of 22 participants gave the same grade to a duplicated assignment across two sessions. Average grade difference was 1.79 for correctness. Conclusion: a shared rubric alone is not sufficient to ensure grading consistency. Download
